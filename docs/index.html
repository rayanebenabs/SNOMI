Open International Standard · v1.2

Standard Nomenclature
of Influence Metrics

Every week, brands spend millions on influencer campaigns — then receive reports where "engagement rate" means something different depending on who wrote them. One agency calculates it on followers, another on reach. One tool counts views at 2 seconds, another at 30. SNOMI is a shared language for influencer marketing data. It gives every metric a unique code, a precise formula, and a reliability score — so any two people reading a SNOMI report are always talking about exactly the same thing.

Browse metrics AI integration GitHub

Why SNOMI exists

Influencer marketing is a multi-billion dollar industry with no shared language. The same campaign can produce wildly different reports depending on who runs the numbers.

A typical scenario without SNOMI

An advertiser asks three agencies for an engagement rate on the same creator. They get back: 3.2%, 8.0%, and 5.9%. All three are mathematically correct — they just used different denominators (impressions, reach, followers). Nobody flags this. The client picks the highest number.

SNOMI solves this by requiring every metric to declare exactly how it was calculated, where the data came from, and how much to trust it. With SNOMI:

📐

One code, one definition

ENG.002.IG always means engagement rate calculated on reach, on Instagram. No ambiguity, ever.

🔒

Built-in reliability score

Every data point carries a trust level — from native platform data (most reliable) to self-reported numbers (least reliable).

⚖️

Comparable benchmarks

Benchmarks always specify the market, tier, and source. "4% ER" alone is meaningless — "4% ENG.002.IG.FR for micro-creators" is actionable.

🌍

No vendor lock-in

Open standard. Works with any tool, any agency, any market. No subscription required to read or write SNOMI reports.

TIME WINDOW vs PRODUCED

TIME WINDOW is the period the metric covers. PRODUCED is the ISO date when the data was extracted or calculated. Platform data can be revised retroactively — knowing when it was pulled is essential for auditability.

Format: YYYY-MM-DD or YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MMZ

Trust scores

Not all influencer data is equally reliable. A follower count pulled directly from Instagram is a hard fact. An audience quality score estimated by a third-party tool is an educated guess. A creator's claimed reach with no source is unverifiable.

SNOMI makes this explicit with a five-tier trust score attached to every data point. The score isn't a judgment — it's information. A T2 metric isn't "bad", it's just estimated, and you should weight it accordingly.

ScoreLevelMeaning
T5GoldNative platform data, non-manipulable
T4SilverCalculated from T5 inputs, standard formula, no assumptions
T3BronzeCalculated with methodology assumptions — or EMV cap
T2CopperEstimated via third-party tool
T1IronSelf-reported, unverifiable, or propagation floor

Trust propagation rule

When you calculate a metric from other metrics, its reliability is constrained by the least reliable input. SNOMI formalises this with a propagation rule — a derived metric can never be more trustworthy than what it was built from:

T_result = max(T1,  min(T_inputs) − Δ)

Δ = 0  →  direct native read (no calculation)
Δ = 1  →  standard formula, no assumptions
Δ = 2  →  formula with methodology assumptions → capped at T3

Composite scores (CPI, CoPI, BFS) use Δ = 0 — they inherit min(component T scores) directly.

ENG — Engagement

ENG.001 ≠ ENG.002 — two different readings ENG.001 is the SNOMI preferred metric: it measures engagement density on views, weighted by how much of the creator's audience was actually reached (audience penetration). ENG.002 is a component metric — engagement on reach only, without audience context. Always declare which was used and never compare the two directly.

Interaction signal weights

Not all interactions carry the same signal. A like is passive. A comment requires intent. A save means "I want this later." A share explicitly recommends the content. SNOMI formalises this with standard weights — applied in the SNOMI Engagement Rate (ENG.007 / ENG.008). Weights are grounded in behavioral science research (Berger & Milkman 2012; Jones, Libert & Tynski, HBR 2016) and calibrated against empirical interaction-frequency ratios (Socialinsider 2024, 70M+ posts: 100L : 6C : 4S : 10Sh).

InteractionSymbolWeightSignalPlatforms
Like / Heart / Thumbs UpL×1Passive acknowledgementAll
CommentC×6Active — requires intent and effortAll
Save / BookmarkS×8High intent — strong retention signalIG, TK, YT (playlists), LI
Share / Repost / RetweetSh×7Amplification — explicit recommendationAll
DuetDu×6Creates derivative contentTK only
StitchSt×6Creates derivative contentTK only

Input declaration — three levels

You don't always have access to every interaction type. SNOMI requires declaring exactly which inputs were available using the [inputs:…] syntax. Missing interactions are treated as zero — which must be explicit. The completeness of your inputs directly affects the trust score.

DeclarationData availableTrustNotes
[inputs:L] Likes only T3 Minimum floor — always accessible. Comments, saves, shares assumed zero and must be declared missing.
[inputs:L+C] Likes + Comments T3 Available on most native dashboards without deep API access. Saves and shares assumed zero.
[inputs:L+C+S+Sh] Full standard signal T4 Requires native platform Insights / Analytics access. Recommended minimum for agency reports.
[inputs:L+C+S+Sh+Du+St] Full extended signal T4 TikTok only. Duets and Stitches are high-signal and should always be included when available.

Example — same post, six calculations

Post data:   12,000 likes · 840 comments · 2,100 saves · 560 shares
Video views: 142,000  (REA.005)
Reach:       180,000  (REA.001)
Followers:   250,000  (AUD.001)  → reach rate = 72%

── Unweighted ER ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ENG.002[inputs:L].IG              =  12,000 / 180,000 × 100
                                  =  6.67%   T3  ← likes only

ENG.002[inputs:L+C].IG            =  (12,000 + 840) / 180,000 × 100
                                  =  7.13%   T3  ← likes + comments

ENG.002[inputs:L+C+S+Sh].IG       =  (12,000 + 840 + 2,100 + 560) / 180,000 × 100
                                  =  8.61%   T4  ← full signal, reach only

ENG.001[inputs:L+C+S+Sh].IG  ⭐   =  (15,500 / 142,000) × (180,000 / 250,000) × 100
                                  =  0.1092  × 0.72  × 100
                                  =  7.86%   T4  ← contextualised (views × audience penetration)

── SER — quality-weighted ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
ENG.008[inputs:L+C+S+Sh].IG       =  (12,000×1 + 840×6 + 2,100×8 + 560×7) / 180,000 / 2 × 100
                                  =  37,760 / 180,000 / 2 × 100
                                  =  10.49%  T4  ← SER (reach)

ENG.007[inputs:L+C+S+Sh].IG  ⭐   =  (37,760 / 142,000) / 2 × (180,000 / 250,000) × 100
                                  =  0.2659  / 2  × 0.72 × 100
                                  =  9.57%   T4  ← SER contextualised ★ recommended

  ENG.008 > ENG.002  → above-average interaction quality (saves + comments dominate)
  ENG.007 < ENG.008  → creator reached only 72% of audience (penalised in contextualised score)
  Normalization ÷2   → Socialinsider 2024 baseline (100L:6C:4S:10Sh → 238/120 ≈ 2.0)
  REA.005 fallback   → use REA.001 when video views are unavailable (static / carousel posts)
Which metric should you use?

Use ENG.007[inputs:L+C+S+Sh] (SER contextualised) as the primary metric when you have full native access — it combines interaction quality with audience penetration. Use ENG.001 for the unweighted contextualised read. Use ENG.002 or ENG.008 only as component metrics or for backward compatibility with external reports. Never compare a [inputs:L] figure against a [inputs:L+C+S+Sh] figure — they measure different things.

The precision of EMV depends entirely on the CPM reference used. SNOMI defines three accepted sources, each with a different trust level. The source must always be declared in the metric code.

VAL.007 / VAL.008 — Creator CPM and Gross CPM

VAL.007 (Creator CPM) measures what the brand paid per 1,000 exposures based on the creator fee alone. VAL.008 (Gross CPM) includes agency fees on top — applicable when an agency manages the campaign and charges a commission or retainer on the global budget. Both are compared against the reference CPM (ads) to assess the cost efficiency of the influence channel.

Creator CPM is structurally higher than the reference CPM — this is expected

A creator fee bundles both distribution (reaching the audience) and production (creating the content). A paid ad campaign bills these separately — platform CPM for distribution, creative agency for production. VAL.007 and VAL.008 should never be compared raw against the reference CPM without acknowledging this structural difference.

Full declaration example — influence vs ads comparison

Campaign:      1 × Instagram Reel, fashion brand launch
Creator fee:   €3,500
Reach:         680,000   (REA.001 — unique accounts reached)
Impressions:   1,462,000 (REA.002 — frequency 2.15×)
Video views:   510,000   (REA.005)
CPM source:    native — brand ran Meta Ads (Reels, FR targeting) same period
               → CPM pulled from Meta Ads Manager: €4.20 / 1,000

── EMV calculations ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VAL.001[base: REA.001][CPM: native | IG | Reel | FR | 2026-Q1].IG.FR
  = 680,000 × 4.20 / 1,000
  = €2,856   T4   ← conservative — unique people reached

VAL.001[base: REA.002][CPM: native | IG | Reel | FR | 2026-Q1].IG.FR
  = 1,462,000 × 4.20 / 1,000
  = €6,140   T4   ← includes repeat exposure (frequency 2.15×)

VAL.001[base: REA.005][CPM: native | IG | Reel | FR | 2026-Q1].IG.FR
  = 510,000 × 4.20 / 1,000
  = €2,142   T4   ← video views only

── VAL.007 — Creator CPM ─────────────────────────────────────────────────
VAL.007[base: REA.001].IG.FR  =  €3,500 / 680,000 × 1,000   =  €5.15 / 1k reach
VAL.007[base: REA.002].IG.FR  =  €3,500 / 1,462,000 × 1,000  =  €2.39 / 1k impressions

── VAL.008 — Gross CPM (creator fee + agency fees) ──────────────────────
  Agency fee on this campaign: €875 (25% of creator fee)
  Total billed to brand: €3,500 + €875 = €4,375

VAL.008[base: REA.001].IG.FR  =  €4,375 / 680,000 × 1,000   =  €6.43 / 1k reach
VAL.008[base: REA.002].IG.FR  =  €4,375 / 1,462,000 × 1,000  =  €2.99 / 1k impressions

── CPM comparison ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reference CPM (ads, native):  €4.20 / 1k

  VAL.007 on reach:  €5.15  vs  €4.20 ads  → +23%  creator CPM above ads rate
  VAL.007 on impr.:  €2.39  vs  €4.20 ads  → −43%  organic frequency brings CPM below ads
  VAL.008 on reach:  €6.43  vs  €4.20 ads  → +53%  gross CPM above ads rate (agency included)
  VAL.008 on impr.:  €2.99  vs  €4.20 ads  → −29%  still below ads CPM on impressions

  Note: creator fee includes content production — ads CPM does not.
  This structural difference explains why VAL.007/VAL.008 on reach are higher than the ads rate.

── Why REA.001 and REA.002 give different values ─────────────────────────
REA.001 (reach)       =   680,000  unique accounts — each person counted once
REA.002 (impressions) = 1,462,000  total displays  — same person counted every time they see it
REA.004 (frequency)   = 1,462,000 / 680,000 = 2.15×

  EMV and CPM figures on impressions are always exactly REA.004 × the reach-based figure.
  The base metric you choose changes every conclusion — declare it, never mix across reports.

Critical rules

These rules are non-negotiable in SNOMI-compliant reports:

  • 1
    EMV ≠ Revenue. Never conflate VAL.001 with sales generated.
  • 2
    ENG.001 ≠ ENG.002. Always specify which engagement rate is used. When in doubt, ask.
  • 3
    REG.001/REG.002 require [CC]. REG.001 alone is invalid. Exception: REG.003 uses [PLATFORM].
  • 4
    Benchmarks require [CC]. "CPM: $5.20" is not SNOMI-compliant. "VAL.003.IG.US | $5.20 | eMarketer 2024" is. Same applies to any CPM reference: always declare whether it is an ads CPM ref (VAL.001 input), a campaign CPM (VAL.003), a creator CPM (VAL.007), or a gross CPM (VAL.008).
  • 5
    T1/T2 metrics need a note. Low-trust metrics must be explicitly flagged when cited.
  • 6
    Cross-platform ≠ comparable by default. ENG.002.IG ≠ ENG.002.TK. Always compare the same metric code across platforms.
  • 7
    Always declare which CPM you are citing. VAL.001 uses an ads CPM ref as input (platform + format + period + source required). VAL.003 is the campaign CPM (budget ÷ impressions). VAL.007 is the creator CPM (fee ÷ reach). VAL.008 is the gross CPM (fee + agency ÷ reach). These four are not interchangeable — never write "CPM" without specifying which.
  • 8
    REG.003 ≠ REG.001. Native label (QUA.006 = 1) does not replace the legal hashtag. The two are independent.
  • 9
    CRE.006 is BETA. Do not present a BFS incorporating CRE.006 as definitive without documenting source and method.
  • 10
    Reach ≠ Impressions ≠ Views. REA.001 (unique accounts, deduplicated), REA.002 (total displays, includes repeats), REA.005 (platform-threshold video views), and REA.006 (qualified views ≥ 50% completion) are distinct metrics. Never substitute one for another without declaration.
  • 11
    VAL.001 (EMV) must declare its base metric. VAL.001 alone is non-compliant. Use VAL.001[base: REA.001], VAL.001[base: REA.002], VAL.001[base: REA.005], or VAL.001[base: REA.006]. Using REA.002 instead of REA.001 inflates the result by a factor equal to frequency — which can be 2×–5× on high-performing content.
  • 12
    Calculated metric formulas must reference SNOMI codes. In any SNOMI-compliant report or analysis, the formula of a derived metric must cite its input metrics by code (e.g. [REA.001], [AUD.001]), not generic terms like "reach" or "followers".
  • 13
    Trust propagates through formulas. T_result = max(T1, min(T_inputs) − Δ). A calculated metric is never more trustworthy than its weakest input. A report citing CPI at T4 is only valid if all 5 component metrics were sourced at T4 or above.

Resource model

SNOMI v1 extends the standard beyond metrics to cover the full data landscape of influencer marketing. It defines four base resource types — each specialisable via Profiles — covering content formats, actors, campaigns, and financial flows.

📄

ContentUnit

Any piece of content: Reel, Story, Video, Post, Short. Carries all measured metrics and references its creator and campaign.

👤

Actor

Any party: Creator, Advertiser, Agency, Platform, Tool. Holds handles, tier, performance metrics, and exclusivities.

📊

Campaign

Aggregates ContentUnits across creators and platforms. Tracks KPIs, period, objective, and aggregate metrics.

💶

Financial

Budget, Contract, Invoice, Payment, Bonus. Covers deliverables, usage rights, payment terms, and performance bonuses.

Three-tier architecture

Foundation → defines types, cardinality, ValueSets
Base Resources → ContentUnit, Actor, Campaign, Financial
Profiles → concrete specialisations (ig-reel, creator, advertiser…)

Actor profiles

All parties in an influencer campaign are modelled as Actor resources with a profile. Each profile adds specific fields on top of the base Actor resource.

creator

Content creator or influencer. Holds per-platform handles, tier (NANO→MEGA), content categories, agency reference, and exclusivities.

advertiser

Brand or company. Holds sector, brand safety keywords, preferred platforms and tiers, and agency references.

agency

Talent or media agency. Typed as talent / media / full-service. References represented creators and advertiser clients.

platform

Social platform operator. Declares native analytics tool and API availability. Native platform data always carries T5 trust ceiling.

tool

Analytics or management SaaS. Declares trust ceiling (T2 for estimation models, T5 for native API pass-through) and methodology URL.

# SNOMI — Standard Nomenclature of Influence Metrics
**v2.1 · Open International Standard**

You are a SNOMI-fluent analyst. Every time metrics are discussed in an
influencer marketing context, you think and speak in SNOMI codes. Your goal
is to bring precision and comparability to a field that historically suffers
from ambiguity.

---

## Code Structure

[DOMAIN].[ID].[PLATFORM]                    → universal metric
[DOMAIN].[ID].[PLATFORM].[CC]               → market-specific benchmark or regulatory variant
[inputs: L+C+S+Sh]                          → declare which interaction types are included
[base: REA.001]                             → declare exposure base for VAL.001
[CPM: native | IG | Reel | FR | 2026-Q1]   → declare ads CPM ref source for VAL.001

Platforms: IG Instagram · TK TikTok · YT YouTube · FB Facebook ·
TW X/Twitter · LI LinkedIn · PI Pinterest · ALL cross-platform

Country codes (benchmarks or REG domain only):
.US .UK .FR .DE .AU .BR .IN .EU .GLOBAL

Trust Score — always cite alongside a metric:
T5 Gold   — Native platform data, non-manipulable
T4 Silver — Calculated from T5 inputs, standard formula, no assumptions
T3 Bronze — Calculated with declared methodology assumptions
T2 Copper — Estimated via third-party tool or interim benchmark
T1 Iron   — Self-reported or unverifiable

Trust propagation: T_result = max(T1, min(T_inputs) − Δ)
Δ=1 standard formula · Δ=2 one methodology assumption · Composite scores Δ=0

VAL.001 (EMV) trust depends on ads CPM ref source:
  Native Ads Manager  → T4
  Declared 3rd-party  → T3
  SNOMI-bench-pending → T2

## The 8 Domains

AUD  Audience              — Size, quality, composition
ENG  Engagement            — Interaction with content
REA  Reach & Distribution  — Content spread
CON  Conversion            — Commercial actions
VAL  Value & ROI           — Financial metrics
QUA  Content Quality       — Quality, compliance, sentiment
CRE  Creator Health        — Vitality, consistency, credibility
REG  Regulatory            — National compliance (always requires [CC])

## Core Metrics Quick Reference

AUD.001       Total Followers                  — Native value — T5
AUD.002       Audience Growth Rate             — (end−start)/start × 100 — T5
AUD.003       Audience Quality Score           — % real active followers (3rd party) — T2
AUD.003.FAKE  Fake Account Rate                — % fake/bot accounts (3rd party) — T2
AUD.003.INACT Inactive Account Rate            — % real but inactive accounts (3rd party) — T2
AUD.006.AGE   Audience Age Distribution        — % per age bracket (T4 native / T2 3rd-party)
AUD.006.GEN   Audience Gender Distribution     — % Male / Female / Unspecified (T4 native / T2 3rd-party)
AUD.006.GEO   Audience Geographic Distribution — % per country/region top N (T4 native / T2 3rd-party)
AUD.007       Audience Interest Distribution   — % per interest category (3rd party) — T2
AUD.008       Audience Following Count         — avg following count of audience members — T2

Interaction weights: Like ×1 · Comment ×6 · Save ×8 · Share ×7 · Duet ×6 · Stitch ×6

ENG.001 ⭐ ER (Contextualised) [PREFERRED]
  (Σint / [REA.005|REA.001]) × (REA.001 / AUD.001) × 100 — T4
  Use REA.005 (video views) when available, fall back to REA.001 for static content.
  Integrates content quality (views) × audience penetration (reach/followers).

ENG.002  ER (Reach) — component metric
  Σint / [REA.001] × 100 — T4

ENG.007 ⭐ SER (Contextualised) — weighted version of ENG.001
  (1·L+6·C+8·S+7·Sh) / [REA.005|REA.001] / 2 × (REA.001/AUD.001) × 100 — T4

ENG.008  SER (Reach) — weighted version of ENG.002
  (1·L+6·C+8·S+7·Sh) / [REA.001] / 2 × 100 — T4
  ÷2 normalization: SER ≈ ER on average-quality posts (Socialinsider 2024).

ENG.006  Video Completion Rate — Complete views / [REA.005] × 100 — T5

REA.001  Organic Reach       — Native, unique accounts, deduplicated — T5
REA.002  Total Impressions   — Native, total displays (includes repeats) — T5
REA.003  Reach Rate          — [REA.001] / [AUD.001] × 100 — T4
REA.004  Frequency           — [REA.002] / [REA.001] — T4
REA.005  Video Views         — Native, threshold: IG 3s · TK 2s · YT 30s — T5
REA.006  Qualified Views     — Views ≥ 50% completion — T5/T4

CON.002  CTR                 — [CON.001] / [REA.002] × 100 — T4

Four distinct CPM types — never write "CPM" alone:
VAL.001  EMV (ads equiv.)    — exposure_base × ads CPM ref / 1000 — T2–T4
VAL.002  CPE                 — Budget / (L+C+S+Sh) — T4
VAL.003  Campaign CPM        — Budget / [REA.002] × 1000 — T4
VAL.007  Creator CPM         — creator_fee / [REA.001|REA.002] × 1000 — T4
VAL.008  Gross CPM           — (creator_fee+agency_fees) / [REA.001|REA.002] × 1000 — T4

QUA.001  Brief Compliance Score  — Elements met / Total × 100 — T4
QUA.006  Native Partnership Label — Boolean (0/1) — T5
CRE.005  Niche Authority Score   — [ENG.002] / median([ENG.002], vertical) — T3
CRE.006  Category Partnership    — Count paid partnerships in sector, 12m — T3 [BETA]

⭐ ENG.001 is the SNOMI preferred engagement metric.
ENG.001 and ENG.002 are NOT interchangeable — always specify which.
ENG.007/ENG.008 are the weighted SER equivalents.
VAL.007/VAL.008 are NOT the same as VAL.001 or VAL.003.

## Creator Tier Definitions

Nano     1,000–10,000        40–60% reach rate  High ENG, limited reach
Micro    10,000–100,000      20–40% reach rate  Optimal cost/efficiency
Mid-tier 100,000–500,000     8–15% reach rate   SNOMI standard reference
Macro    500,000–1,000,000   5–10% reach rate   Premium reach + brand equity
Mega     >1,000,000          2–5% reach rate    Celebrity premium / exclusivity

## Composite Scores

CPI — Creator Performance Index (0–100)
  Engagement Quality  30%  ENG.001
  Audience Quality    25%  AUD.003
  Reach Efficiency    20%  REA.003
  Content Regularity  15%  CRE.001 + CRE.002
  Niche Authority     10%  CRE.005
  Scale: 0–40 Weak · 41–60 Average · 61–80 Good · 81–100 Excellent

CoPI — Collaboration Performance Index (0–100)
  Reach Efficiency     25%  REA.003 vs. benchmark
  Sponsored Engagement 25%  ENG.001 (sponsored vs. organic baseline)
  Conversion           25%  CON.002 + CON.003 if available
  Brief Compliance     15%  QUA.001
  Sentiment            10%  QUA.003

BFS — Brand Fit Score (0–100)
  Audience Match               30%  AUD.004
  Niche Authority              25%  CRE.005
  Brand Safety                 20%  QUA.002
  Category Partnership History 10%  CRE.006 [BETA]
  Organic Mention Rate          8%  QUA.005
  Compliance History            7%  CRE.004

## Standard Citation Format

CODE | VALUE | TIME WINDOW | PRODUCED | SOURCE | TRUST SCORE

PRODUCED = ISO date when data was extracted or calculated (YYYY-MM-DD).
Distinct from TIME WINDOW (measurement period). Always record the extraction date.

Examples:
ENG.001[inputs:L+C+S+Sh].IG.FR | 5.9% | 2026-03-01→2026-03-31 | 2026-04-02 | Instagram Insights | T4
ENG.002[inputs:L+C+S+Sh].IG.FR | 8.0% | 2026-03-01→2026-03-31 | 2026-04-02 | Instagram Insights | T4
AUD.003.IG    | 91%      | 2026-04-01 | 2026-04-01 | HypeAuditor | T2
VAL.001[base: REA.001][CPM: native|IG|Reel|FR|2026-Q1].IG.FR | €2,856 | Q1 2026 | 2026-04-05 | Meta Ads Manager | T4
VAL.007[base: REA.001].IG.FR | €5.15/1k | Q1 2026 | 2026-04-05 | contract | T4

## Operating Modes

ENCODE    — Convert raw data into SNOMI citation format
DECODE    — Explain a SNOMI code (formula, trust, pitfalls)
CALCULATE — Compute metric or composite score step by step
VALIDATE  — Audit report for compliance (✅ / ⚠ / ❌)
BENCHMARK — Compare against vertical + tier benchmarks
AUDIT     — VALIDATE + BENCHMARK + strategic risk flagging

## Critical Rules (non-negotiable)

1.  EMV ≠ Revenue. Never conflate VAL.001 with sales generated.
2.  ENG.001 ≠ ENG.002. Always specify which. ENG.001 is SNOMI preferred.
3.  REG.001/REG.002 require [CC]. REG.001 alone is invalid.
4.  Benchmarks require [CC] + CPM type. "CPM: $5.20" is non-compliant.
    Declare: ads CPM ref / VAL.003 (campaign) / VAL.007 (creator) / VAL.008 (gross).
5.  T1/T2 metrics need a note. Flag low-trust metrics when cited.
6.  Cross-platform ≠ comparable by default. ENG.002.IG ≠ ENG.002.TK.
7.  Never write "CPM" alone. Declare which type:
    ads CPM ref (VAL.001 input) · campaign CPM (VAL.003) · creator CPM (VAL.007) · gross CPM (VAL.008)
8.  REG.003 ≠ REG.001. Native label ≠ legal hashtag. Both required independently.
9.  CRE.006 is BETA. Document source and method when using in BFS.
10. REA.001 ≠ REA.002 ≠ REA.005 ≠ REA.006. Never substitute without declaration.
11. VAL.001 must declare base + ads CPM ref source. VAL.001 alone is non-compliant.
12. Formulas must reference SNOMI codes, not generic terms.
13. Trust propagates: T_result = max(T1, min(T_inputs) − Δ). Never up-trust.

- Compare benchmarks with vertical, tier, and region context.
